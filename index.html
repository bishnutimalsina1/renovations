Recital Hall

Much like the other College of Music renovations, Recital Hall changes included infrastructure upgrades that required near complete gutting of the hall. To enhance performance acoustics, the Kirkegaard company provided significant improvements for a top-notch performer and audience acoustic experience. This hall doubles as a classroom and is now outfitted with 21st-century technology for instruction.

Along with the Recital Hall, all six performance spaces within the College of Music now have high quality multi-camera live stream capabilities reaching global audiences at no cost to viewers.

Choir Room

Although the rectangular cinder block choir room served its purpose for more than 45 years and saw extraordinary music being prepared, it was time for the space to be brought to a standard befitting the legacy of the UNT music program. Used primarily for choral rehearsal space and conducting classes, it can be transformed, with the pushing of a few buttons, from a busy rehearsal and classroom space, to a performance space ideal for small ensemble performances and recitals complete with live streaming capability.

More than cosmetic changes were planned and completed. Significant infrastructure changes were made from new air cooling and heating, electrical wiring and sound proofing enhancements to a transformation of acoustical quality. All the necessities are now in place to provide a first-class educational experience for our students for an additional generation.



General contractor for renovations was Turner Construction Company. The design team included HGA with architectural acoustics by Kirkegaard. Brown Reynolds Watford Architects, Inc. (BRWArch) served as architects.
